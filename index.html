Current neural audio codecs, such as SpeechTokenizer, have demonstrated strong performance in speech reconstruction and feature disentanglement. However, their application to zero-shot voice conversion remains challenging due to the persistent entanglement of speaker identity and linguistic content. In this paper, we propose a novel audio codec designed to explicitly decouple speaker identities from semantic content. Our approach introduces two synergistic strategies: (i) a neural VC-based data augmentation scheme with cross-reconstruction objectives, and (ii) novel temporal residual shuffling that disrupts sequential dependencies to enforce the aggregation of global timbre characteristics. Our model achieves a 37.4% relative reduction in WER over the baseline, while maintaining high-fidelity reconstruction quality. Subjective tests further confirm that our method is significantly preferred, underscoring the efficacy of temporal disruption for robust disentanglement in codec training.
